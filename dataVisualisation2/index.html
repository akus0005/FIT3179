Formula 1: World's Most Prestigious Car Racing Event

Analysis of Formula 1 Grand Prix from 2015-2019

.
Held since 1950, Formula One (can be written as Formula 1 or F1) is an annual single-seater racing competition. It is one of the world's highest class of sport racing. Each year (or season) consists of a series of races (also known as Grand Prix) that take place across the globe. An average of 10 teams/constructors with 20 drivers compete to gain the title of World Champion. Although the sport mainly based in Europe, it also takes place in other continents, including Australia, Asia, and America. The Drivers

Drivers are one of the key component in the success of Formula 1. Every season, each teams are given a maximum of 2 people as their main drivers to compete and a reserve driver. While main drivers compete on track, reserve driver is there to practice on practice sessions or standby incase something emergency happened.
